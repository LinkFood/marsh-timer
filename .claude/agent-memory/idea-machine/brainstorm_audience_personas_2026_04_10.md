## 10 Specific Personas (April 10, 2026)

### The Compound Context Gap
Every persona has access to SOME data. None have it fused. The value = cross-reference.

### The "Compared to What?" Problem
Historical comparison is the universal need, not current state. Time machine = core value prop.

### The Credibility Chain
Self-grading loop matters most to consequential-decision personas (emergency mgr, insurance, crop adjuster, water utility). Grading loop = compliance, not marketing.

### Revenue Mapping
- Cat bond analyst: $1-5K/mo (API, embed in modeling pipeline)
- Crop insurance adjuster: $200/report or $100/mo seat
- Litigation support: $200/report (replaces $400 forensic met reports)
- Renewable energy assessor: $500/site screening (replaces $15-50K formal assessment as pre-screen)
- Water utility operator: $50/mo (50K systems in US = massive TAM)
- Event insurance underwriter: $100/mo (faster policy pricing = more commissions)
- Emergency manager: $50/mo (dept budget, FEMA compliance driver)
- TV weather producer: $200/mo (speed of analog lookup)
- Journalist: $50/mo (credits product in stories = organic marketing)
- Phenology hobbyist: $10/mo or free (viral sharing, naturalist networks)

### Go-To-Market Notes
- Journalist + phenology hobbyist = viral/brand awareness tier
- Emergency manager + water utility = institutional adoption tier (50K+ potential orgs)
- Insurance/legal/energy = high-revenue B2B tier (long sales cycle)
- Trial lawyer slip-and-fall = highest volume transactional ($200/report x 400K cases/yr)
