# 1. THE CONTENTS INVENTORY — what's actually inside the American almanac form

Sources: Old Farmer's Almanac (OFA, Dublin NH, est. 1792, ~3M circ) and Farmers' Almanac (FA, Lewiston ME, est. 1818, relaunched 3/2026). Verified via [Wikipedia (OFA)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Old_Farmer%27s_Almanac), [Wikipedia (FA)](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Farmers%27_Almanac), [almanac.com 2026 edition listings](https://www.almanac.com/2026-old-farmers-almanac), [store.almanac.com](https://store.almanac.com/products/old-farmers-almanac), the [2022 OFA chapter structure](https://zoboko.com/text/eno945q4/the-old-farmers-almanac-2022/15), [farmersalmanac.com](https://www.farmersalmanac.com/), and section pages linked per-row.

## A. The astronomical spine ("calendar of the heavens")

| Section | What it is | The job it does | Data or content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Left-Hand Calendar Pages** (12, one per month) | Daily table: sunrise/sunset, length of day, sun fast, moonrise/moonset, moon's age, moon's astronomical place, **Boston high-tide times**, day of year | THE ritual page — the daily consult; readers keep it by the door. It's a daily, dated, place-fitted reading | Pure data (computed ephemeris) |
| **Right-Hand Calendar Pages** (facing) | Per-day: feasts/holy days, civil holidays, historical events, weather-prediction doggerel verse, tide heights, proverbs, and the **Farmer's Calendar essay** (a short nature/rural-life meditation per month) | Lore + delight woven into the same dated grid; the poetry that makes the data feel alive ([almanac.com engravings page](https://www.almanac.com/engravings-old-farmers-almanac-calendar-pages)) | Content anchored to data rows |
| **Sky Watch / month-by-month astronomy** | Narrative of what's visible each month: planet positions, conjunctions, bright stars | Go-outside-tonight prompts | Data (ephemeris) + editorial gloss |
| **Eclipses** | Year's solar/lunar eclipses, visibility | Event anticipation, mark-the-calendar | Data |
| **Meteor showers** | Annual shower table: dates, radiant, best viewing, hourly rates | Ritual night out (Perseids in August) | Data |
| **Full moon dates + names** | The 12-13 full moons with traditional names (Wolf, Harvest, Beaver…), largely Native American/Colonial lineage ([almanac.com/full-moon-names](https://www.almanac.com/full-moon-names)) | The single most viral almanac artifact — media reprints them monthly | Data + named lore |
| **Planet visibility tables** | Rise/set/transit for visible planets through the year | Planning observation | Data |
| **Bright stars / North Star features** | Star transit times, navigation lore | Wonder + orientation | Data + content |
| **Twilight / dawn-dusk table** | Civil/astronomical twilight lengths by latitude band | Hunters, photographers, farmers — usable-light planning | Data |
| **Tide tables + Tide Corrections** | Boston tides daily, plus a correction table converting Boston to ~hundreds of other coastal stations; glossary of tide terms (spring, neap, syzygy) | Coastal readers' daily utility; the correction table is the "fitted to YOUR ground" move | Data |
| **Time Corrections table** | Convert Boston sun/moon times to ~hundreds of US/Canadian cities by longitude/latitude keys | Same move: one national table, personalized locally | Data |
| **Chronological cycles & eras** | Golden Number, Epact, Dominical Letter, year in other calendars (Jewish, Chinese, Islamic) | Deep-time flavor; "this book knows all the calendars" | Data (calendrical) |

## B. Weather (the famous part)

| Section | What it is | The job | Data or content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Long-range forecasts, 16 US regions** (FA: 7 zones) | Season-by-season and month-by-month temp/precip vs normal, written up to 18 months ahead | The headline product; regional identity ("what's OUR winter") | Claimed data ("secret formula" — Robert B. Thomas 1792 solar-cycle formula; FA's "Caleb Weatherbee" pseudonymous forecaster) |
| **The accuracy self-report** | An annual page: "How accurate was our forecast last winter?" — self-graded, traditionally claiming ~80% (last winter self-reported 86.1%; independent studies find ~50-52%) ([almanac.com accuracy page](https://www.almanac.com/how-accurate-old-farmers-almanacs-weather-forecast), [SciAm](https://www.scientificamerican.com/article/can-you-trust-farmers-almanacs-weather-predictions/)) | Trust theater — readers love that it grades itself, even generously. **This is DCD's court, done dishonestly, for 230 years** | Content dressed as data |
| **Weather folklore & signs in nature** | Proverbs, prediction rhymes, "20 Signs of a Hard Winter" (woolly bear bands, persimmon seeds, thick corn husks, early geese) ([farmersalmanac.com](https://www.farmersalmanac.com/20-signs-of-a-hard-winter-ahead)) | The most shared/talked-about content; porch conversation fuel | Pure lore (some testable!) |
| **Weather history / extremes essays** | Great storms, record years, retrospectives | Story + memory | Content over data |
| **Weather glossary** | Terms, cloud types, wind scales | Reference | Content |

## C. The moon-timing engine (the beloved ritual core)

| Section | What it is | The job | Data or content |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Best Days timetable** | For ~40+ activities, the 2-3 best days per month by the moon's zodiac sign + phase: plant above-ground crops, plant root crops, cut hair (to grow/to slow), quit smoking, start a diet, wean, castrate, set eggs, dig post holes, jar jams, mow to slow growth, go camping, host a party, potty-train… ([almanac.com/bestdays/timetable](https://www.almanac.com/bestdays/timetable), [FA Best Days](https://www.farmersalmanac.com/calendar/best-days-calendar-2)) | The #1 reader ritual — people schedule haircuts and surgeries by it. Grow tasks between new→full moon; slow tasks full→new | Astrological tradition presented as timing data |
| **Best Fishing Days** | The days between new and full moon each month, plus solunar-style feeding times ([almanac.com/best-fishing-days](https://www.almanac.com/best-fishing-days)) | The fisherman's page; second-most consulted table | Lore with a moon-data skeleton |
| **The Man of Signs / Secrets of the Zodiac** | The anatomical zodiac figure (Aries=head … Pisces=feet) + the moon's astrological place table; historically used to time medical procedures, livestock work, sauerkraut ([almanac.com/man-signs-zodiac-man](https://www.almanac.com/man-signs-zodiac-man)) | The oldest page in the form (predates the OFA itself); pure heritage talisman | Astrology, explicitly labeled tradition by OFA itself |
| **Gardening by the Moon calendar** | Planting calendar merging frost dates + moon phase/sign | Bridges the "real" gardening data and the lore | Hybrid |

|---|---|---|---|

| **Home remedies & natural cures** | Herbal/folk health features | Self-reliance identity | Content |

**The meta-insight:** the almanac is ~40% computed ephemeris tables fitted to your place, ~20% moon-timing ritual, ~15% weather claims + self-grading, ~25% lore/food/humor community glue. The form's genius is the WEAVE — every lore item is pinned to a dated, computed row. That's exactly DCD's architecture already.

# 3. TOP 8 ADDITIONS (reader-ritual value × buildability × never-forecast fit)

1. **Frost dates + growing season, per state, from DCD's own 76 years** — the almanac's most-used data page, computable today from `ghcn-daily` rows already owned, and DCD can do what no almanac can: show the distribution ("last spring frost in VA: median Apr 14, earliest Mar 20, latest May 9, from 27,000 station-days") instead of a single date. Build: one script computing first/last 32°F crossings per state-year → new content_type + a `/frost` surface + Morning Line sentence in season.
2. **The daily almanac panel (Left-Hand Page reborn)** — sunrise/sunset/day-length/moon rise-set-age-phase/tide/solunar majors for YOUR state on the Morning Line, every day. All lanes exist (photoperiod, Schlyter, solunar, tide); this is assembly + the ritual frame. This is the single highest-leverage move: it makes the daily visit a ritual, not a check.
3. **Best Fishing Days, graded** — reframe the live solunar lane as the named almanac table ("this month's best days"), then close the loop no almanac ever dared: grade it against eBird/acoustic/activity signals in the court. Build: monthly rollup view over `hunt_solunar_calendar` + a grading claim type.
4. **Full moon names + moon calendar page** — trivial (static names × computed phases), disproportionate shareability; it's the almanac content the press reprints monthly. Build: names table + `/moon` page off the Schlyter lane.
5. **Folklore-on-trial** — ingest the public-domain proverb corpus ("woolly bear," "halo around the moon," "thick corn husks") as a `folklore` lane, then test each against the archive and print the verdict with receipts. LABELED LORE + GRADE is the perfect DCD-native carry of the almanac's most-loved content — lore as hypotheses, never claims. Build: ~200-proverb seed file → embed → retrodiction harness (Lookout Mine pattern) → verdict cards.
6. **Meteor showers + eclipses + aurora watch surface** — data all in hand (`backfill-astronomical`, `hunt-space-weather` Kp); render "Perseids peak Tuesday; last 10 Augusts, VA sky was clear 6 of 10" — anticipation without forecasting (base rates, not predictions). Build: `/sky` block + Morning Line line on event days.
7. **Planting calendar (frost + crop offsets)** — rides on #1; static crop-offset table × per-state frost climatology = "tomatoes out after May 3 in MD (90% safe by your own 76 years)." The almanac's gardening spine with receipts.
8. **Seasons calendar surface (hunt_seasons)** — the data is sitting unsurfaced: every species/state/zone/date/bag-limit. A dated "what opens this week in your state" strip is pure almanac ("calendar of the year") and pure DCD brand. Build: route + weekly Morning Line mention.

(Runner-up: holidays/moveable-feasts + Glossary-of-Oddities-style calendar lore on /date — cheap Wikimedia ingest, high texture.)

# 4. THE ASTROLOGY / BEST DAYS TENSION — for the owner to rule on

**The fact:** the Best Days timetable and Man of Signs are arguably the almanac's most beloved ritual feature — people schedule haircuts, weaning, and diets by the moon's zodiac sign. It's also the one section that is astrology, full stop: moon-sign placement has no physical mechanism, and the house law is never-astrology, every-claim-graded.

**Framings available, fair to both sides:**
- **Option A — refuse entirely** (current de facto state; grep shows zero zodiac code, and BornPage already chose real astronomy over signs). Cleanest law; forfeits the single stickiest ritual in the form.
- **Option B — carry the real skeleton, drop the zodiac skin.** The Best Days table is ~half real astronomy: moon PHASE (new→full for growth tasks) and actual moon position. DCD already computes both. Publish "moon days" timing built only from phase + solunar + photoperiod — the ritual utility without a single astrological claim.
- **Option C — LABELED TRADITION lane.** Print the Man of Signs and moon-sign Best Days as what they are: a 400-year-old timing tradition, typographically fenced as LORE, never entering the court, never in a claim sentence — the way OFA itself now frames the Man of Signs as heritage. Maximum form-fidelity; risk is brand bleed ("they do astrology now").
- **Option D — lore-on-trial (the DCD-native move):** carry the tradition AND grade it. "Tradition says plant root crops in a dark, earthy sign. We checked your 76 years. Verdict: no signal (n=…)." Honors the form, feeds the court, converts the almanac's mysticism into DCD's honesty engine. Highest character; some readers may not want their ritual graded.

This is the owner's call. Everything else in the inventory is buildable inside existing law.
