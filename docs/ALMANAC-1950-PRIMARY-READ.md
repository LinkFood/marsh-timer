> **THIS DOCUMENT SUPERSEDES `docs/ALMANAC-INVENTORY-2026-07-17.md`** for everything the 1950 form can teach. ## 2. THE RECURRING TABLES — exact columns

### 2a. Left-hand calendar page (×12) — "ASTRONOMICAL CALCULATIONS"
Header: `1950] MONTH, Nth Month.` Three furniture blocks above the grid:
1. **Sun's Declination mini-table** (Days | ° ′, five column-pairs spanning the month — the equinox sign-flip is visible raw in March/September)
2. **Moon-phase block** (each phase: glyph, day, h m, morning/evening, E/W letter — May 1950 carries FIVE lines for its two full moons)
3. Standing rubric: **"KEY LETTERS REFER TO CORRECTIONS TABLE, PAGE 48, FOR ALL POINTS OUTSIDE NEW ENGLAND"**

The grid, 15 columns exactly:

| # | Column |
|---|---|
| 1 | Day of Year (1–365 running) |
| 2 | Day of Month |
| 3 | Day of Week (Sundays bold) |
| 4 | ☉ Rises h.m. |
| 5 | **Key** (letter A–Q → p.48) |
| 6 | ☉ Sets h.m. |
| 7 | **Key** |
| 8 | Length of Days h.m. |
| 9 | **Sun Fast m.** (sundial→clock correction) |
| 10–11 | Full Sea, Boston — Morn / Even (tide TIMES, fractional hours) |
| 12 | ☽ Rises (or Sets) h.m. — the word "rises"/"sets" printed inline where the column flips at phase boundaries; dashes for no-event days |
| 13 | **Key** |
| 14 | ☽ Souths h.m. (transit) |
| 15 | ☽'s Place (**constellation** abbreviations: CNC, LEO, VIR, LIB, SCO, SGR, CAP, AQR, PSC, ARI, TAU, G'M) + Moon's Age |

### 2b. Right-hand calendar page (×12)
Header: `MONTH hath N days. [1950`. Furniture top to bottom:
1. **Zodiac-animal engraving** (oval, foliage)
2. **A real lyric poem** (uncredited modern verse — a different register from the doggerel)
3. The grid: **D.M. | D.W. | "Aspects, Holidays, Heights of High Water, Weather, etc." | Farmer's Calendar**

The third column is a per-day micro-collage, all in ONE typographic column: church calendar (movable feasts, saints), civil holidays, historical anniversaries (Pompeii A.D. 79 to "Pollsters eat crow 1948" at equal rank), **tide HEIGHTS in braces {10.8/9.3} ft** (times are on the left page — the tide is split across the spread, binding it), astronomical aspects in glyphs, phenology-and-hazard lines pinned to dates ("Maple sap runs now," "Shad running Conn. River," "Swallows arr. Capistrano," "Set your hens on the 24th," "Chimney fire danger now," "Coldest in yr. 14th–25th," "Watch for shooting stars 9th to 11th"), weather memory pinned to holidays ("Easter Day — Snowed 9″ 1917"), fertility/folk lore ("Girls beware of the red ear," "Male child most likely if conceived now"), and — threaded down the month in italics — **the weather forecast as one continuous enjambed doggerel poem, sharded 2–4 words per day** ("Hot for the season, / rains beyond reason…"). The glossary (p.117) documents this mechanic explicitly: the forecast covers "three or four days time as shown by the spread of the words down the page."

Fourth column: the **Farmer's Calendar essay** — a topical serial, not timeless meditation: land surveys (Jan), chimney fires (Feb), sugaring (Mar), hail insurance with actual rates (Jul), postwar labor (Aug), bran-mash recipe with quantities (Oct), and December signs off blessing the reader.

Footer: a **rotating one-line lore item**, different each month: "SET, CUT, SOW / SPRING OR GROW / IN THE INCREASE OF THE MOON" (Feb) · "Last Qtr.—New Moon—Phases Best Fishing" (Apr) · "If bees swarm, ring an alarm" (Jun) · "Next month always has 19 fine days" (Sep) · the Hood "No fruits, no flowers…" quote (Nov). **In 1950, Best Days / Best Fishing is tiny footer furniture, not a named table.**

### 2c. The localization system (not a section — a system)
- **Key letters** on every sun/moon time row → **p.48 matrix**: ~29 cities × columns A–Q + a moon constant, signed minutes, interpolate between nearest two, ±5 min anywhere in the U.S.
- New England towns: East/West minutes mini-table on p.21.
- Planting tables: inline rule — "add one week every 100 miles north or 500 feet elevation" — 3 latitudes cover the country.
- Tides: p.100, ~70 stations, columns = Station | Time Difference h.m. (±) | Height Difference Ft. (± or *ratio to multiply Boston's height), worked example, cross-refs by page and column number.
- Twilight: p.117 table, latitude band × date range → h m; subtract from sunrise for dawn, add to sunset for dark.
- Weather: one sentence — Boston's forecast, minus one day per time zone west.

### 2d. Other recurring tables, columns verified
- **Planting (pp.8–10):** Crop (with E/L splits) | Jan–Dec symbol timeline (`*****`/`ooooo`/`xxxxx`) | Seeds Per Acre | 1950 Moon Most Favorable (a date).
- **Game laws (pp.11–13, 105–106):** State+Species | Season dates | Bag limit (daily; italics = seasonal), symbols ♂ † ‡ #, footnote classes for Maine waters, "No closed season" / "Dates not set" / "Closed" as first-class values.
- **Gestation (p.14):** Animal | Proper age first mating | Reproductive years | Females per male | Gestation/incubation **Shortest / Mean / Longest days** — a distribution, printed in 1950.
- **Frosts (p.14):** City | Last spring frost | First fall frost (~34 cities).
- **Planets (p.6):** Month-day (1/11/21) | rise-or-set h.m. per planet | Key letter each.
- **Motor vehicle laws (p.84):** State | Speed limit (R=reasonable) | New-plate date | License min. age | Gas tax | Sales tax % | Period of stay | Safety-responsibility law y/n | Title required y/n; 13 footnotes.
- **Lunation weather table (p.77):** Moon-change hour band (11 bands) | If in Summer | If in Winter; 3 footnote rules; source "Farmers & Mechanics Manual, 1874"; cross-ref "see left hand calendar pages."
- **Bird arrivals (p.50):** Species | arrival DATE RANGE, attributed to Mass. Audubon.
- **Postal (pp.109–110):** class × zone × weight rate grids; C.O.D. fee brackets; foreign tiers.

## 3. THE WEAVE — voice, furniture, interleave

1. **No fences.** Saints, tide heights, Ussher's 4004 B.C., Gandhi's assassination, husking-bee fertility lore, and the Perseids share one column with equal typographic authority. A medium locating a runaway boy prints beside an AAA law table. The 1950 form is NOT scrupulous about lore-vs-fact — the reader is trusted to sort it. (This is where DCD deliberately improves on the form rather than copies it.)
2. **The voice is deadpan understatement stitched into data:** "He was a very strong man." · Election Day weather: "Democratic." · index entry "Boy, lost…78" · glossary entry for Rain. Jokes live INSIDE data cells and reference apparatus, not in a humor ghetto.
3. **Cross-reference is the connective tissue.** Nearly every table points at another page by number (key letters → p.48; lunation table → "left hand calendar pages"; tide example → "page 28, column 11"; index → the decoder pages; captions centralized on p.17 for plates 80+ pages away; puzzles → answers on p.114; game laws split 13→105). The book is a hyperlinked object that forces traversal — through the ads.
4. **Receipts everywhere.** Tables carry source credits (AAA, Outdoors Magazine with its price, Mass. Audubon, Forbes, Boston Cultivator 1846, "Farmers & Mechanics Manual, 1874," Visher/AMS) and printed epistemic hedges (holiday confidence stars, game-law press-date caveat, migratory-bird staleness warning, postal pending-legislation note, a NULL occultation report). The 1950 book already practiced receipts-law — for everything except its weather formula.
5. **The claim-verdict spread.** P.7 fuses last winter's self-grade (with dated outcome-anecdotes as evidence AND an admitted miss) with next winter's forecast in one page. The grading grammar is event-receipts: snow fleas on Feb 26 as verification.
6. **Ads are load-bearing.** ~⅓ of pages; a 14-page sponsor insert disguised as editorial; advertisers writing in the book's voice (John Hancock) or literally cloning its page grammar (Jordan Marsh's calendar-page ad); an ad selling readers a rain gauge with an 11-year record book; and the publisher's-conscience PEN PALS box fencing a dangerous ad on its own page. Commerce and editorial interleave at paragraph scale.
7. **Persona-as-infrastructure.** Abe Weatherwise, the facsimile Thomas signature 104 years after his death, named staff with towns, reader Q&A by initials+town, contests with winners' names printed. The community mechanisms are typographic.
8. **Physical rhythm.** Three-photo wordless interludes; landscape rotations when content wants width; articles running off pages mid-sentence; three unrelated tables stacked with no transition; a repeated banner law (STANDARD TIME…) at front and back. Density itself is the aesthetic — and the book documents its own mechanics (the glossary defines how to read the sharded forecast).

## 4. THE DIFF — primary source vs. the web draft (`ALMANAC-INVENTORY-2026-07-17.md`)

### What the draft got RIGHT (confirmed against the object)
- The left/right calendar-page split and their jobs; Boston-fitted times + corrections concept; movable feasts + chronological cycles; Farmer's Calendar essay per month; month engravings.
- The accuracy self-report as the form's trust-theater (and DCD's court parallel) — confirmed, and stronger than the draft knew (see below).
- Holidays, eclipses, planet visibility, gestation/mating tables (column structure verified), frost-date city table, planting tables, hunting/fishing digest, tide corrections, glossary, amusements, reader-community mechanisms, ads-as-texture. All present in 1950.
- The meta-insight — "every lore item is pinned to a dated, computed row" — is validated, and made literal by the lunation table's printed cross-reference into the ephemeris.

### What the draft got WRONG (for 1950)
- **Best Days / Best Fishing Days as big named tables** — in 1950 they are one-line footer furniture under the calendar ("Last Qtr.—New Moon—Phases Best Fishing") plus a "Moon Most Favorable" date column inside the planting table. The named timetables are later-era growth. (Draft §C and gap-map rows describe the modern form, not the original.)
- **The Man of Signs as a beloved centerpiece** — in 1950 it is a numbered key on the decoder page, no figure; and the book's own glossary calls the zodiac the basis of "some astrology — and superstition." The 1950 almanac was cooler toward astrology than the draft (or the modern OFA) implies. This materially softens the draft's §4 "astrology tension": the ORIGINAL form already leaned real-astronomy.
- **Full moon names** — absent from 1950 entirely (moons appear as Harvest Moon/Hunters Moon calendar entries only). A modern-era artifact.
- **16-region long-range forecast** — 1950's method is Boston's forecast minus one day per time zone west, in one sentence. Regionalization came later.
- **"The General Store / classifieds" as quaint texture** — advertising is ~⅓ of the book, structurally load-bearing, including a 14-page native-advertising insert and ads that clone the almanac's own grammar.
- **Planting tables "keyed to region/zip"** — 1950 uses 3 latitudes + an interpolation rule.
- **The hole** — no punched corner on this copy; not a 1950 feature (draft's claim is modern-form).
- **"Weather-prediction doggerel verse" per day** — it is ONE continuous poem sharded down the month; you can't read a day's forecast without the month's sentence. The glossary documents the mechanic.

### What the draft COMPLETELY MISSED (the primary-source hauls)
1. **The Key-letter correction system** — per-row letters on every time column → one national matrix page, taught by two pages of worked arithmetic. "Fitted to your ground" is a *system threaded through every table*, not a section.
2. **The claim+verdict single page** (p.7) with dated outcome-anecdotes as receipts and an admitted miss.
3. **The sharded weather poem** and the two distinct registers of verse (lyric poem vs. doggerel thread).
4. **The phenology-and-hazard layer** woven per-date into the calendar ("Maple sap runs now," "Shad running Conn. River," "Chimney fire danger now," "Coldest in yr. 14th–25th") — the most DCD-native feature in the book, and the draft had no row for it.
5. **Printed epistemic furniture** — confidence stars, null results, staleness caveats, source credits with prices, the PEN PALS conscience box. The 1950 form already had honesty furniture everywhere except the forecast itself.
6. **The lunation weather table** — fully-specified testable folklore (moon-change hour × season → weather) with 1874 provenance and a cross-ref into the book's own ephemeris. A ready-made folklore-on-trial defendant.
7. **The human-referral directory** (48 state extension directors by name), **"Scientists at Work"** (skeptical ag-science digest incl. the 1949 DDT warning), the **cartoonist residency**, the **general reference desk** (motor law, postal, measures, herbs, hints), **photo interludes**, the **cypher contest**, **distribution-not-point data** (gestation Shortest/Mean/Longest; bird arrivals as ranges), the **World Calendar advocacy page**, and the **back-of-book INDEX as the only ToC** — with jokes in it.
8. **Structural weave mechanics:** sections split across the book on purpose; tide data split across the spread (times left, heights right); cross-references as connective tissue; advertisers writing into the form.

## 5. REVISED BUILD IMPLICATIONS — what the real 1950 object teaches (1950-form findings)

These refine, and where noted reorder, the draft's Top-8. Everything here is a 1950-form finding; modern-form rulings still await James's physical copy.
