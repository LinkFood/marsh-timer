## Competitive landscape (verified via web sweep, 2026-07-17 — sources in the session record)

**VERDICT: the seat is empty.** Nothing combines live-physics triggers + attached historical
receipts + public self-grading + the almanac form. Each piece exists in isolation; nobody is
even building toward the fusion.

- **Urgency signal:** the 208-year Farmers' Almanac went broke Nov 2025, was rescued, and
  relaunched March 2026 by a content-media operator as "a living, breathing publication with
  fresh, daily content" — the POSITIONING LANGUAGE is now claimed by a company with no engine.
  They took the words; the machine is unclaimed.
- **Old Farmer's Almanac** (Yankee, ~2.5-3M circ, profitable) is publicly ANTI-AI content —
  a moat marker, not a threat. **Semios rebranded as "Almanac"** (agtech unicorn, B2B ag ops)
  — owns the AI+almanac press association but a different seat entirely.
- **The unoccupied differentiators:** (i) trigger-plus-receipts ("the last 19 times...") —
  consumer-facing analog/similar-day exists nowhere outside academic tools; (ii) public
  self-grading of one's own narrative claims — only third-party graders (ForecastAdvisor)
  exist; (iii) the never-forecast "what's forming" frame — unused by anyone.
- **Category sizing, honest:** IBM paid $2.28B for Weather Co digital (2016), sold it for
  ~$1.1B (2024) — the flagship halved. AccuWeather ~$1B-class. Almanac-as-print monetizes in
  single-digit millions and went broke at 2.1M circulation. Weather ATTENTION is where
  nine-figure value lives; the almanac FORM is the wedge, not the business.
- **Naming guardrails:** "almanac" is generic and safe as a descriptor ("the living almanac
  of American ground" ✓). NEVER name the product "___ Almanac" (three claimants: Yankee's
  registered marks + litigated yellow trade dress; Farmers' Almanac LLC relaunching; Semios's
  almanac.ag). Never "Farmer's" anything. No yellow covers.
