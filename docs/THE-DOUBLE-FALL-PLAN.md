# DCD: The Plan That Matches Your Head

---

## 1. THE GAP

The vision's verb is **falling** — from orbit to your creek, from today into October 1961 — and the current surface has no fall in it anywhere. Nothing descends, nothing travels, nothing lands. You click a flat abbreviation box and a card *appears* in a side rail, the way every dashboard since 2019 has appeared. The signature shiver — "the machine can name the date" — is replaced by four rows of same-week days with similar highs, which is the archive agreeing that November resembles November. And at the exact climax, when a rhyme date should pull you down a thread into 1968, the app teleports you to a generic archive browser and the story dies. The feel that's missing isn't "more layers." It's **consequence and gravity**: the sense that this thing *remembers out loud*, in dated sentences with receipts, and that the screen is a place you enter — not a form that fills. Right now 7.6M rows of memory are wearing a weather widget's clothes.

## 2. THE MOVE

**One bet: replace dashboard grammar with camera-and-sentence grammar. The hero experience is the Double Fall — down through space, then down through time.**

Open the app: one enormous dated sentence about the continent, set in the brand serif. Tap your state: the camera *rushes in* — 49 states falling away dim, real point data materializing below the clouds. And the landing isn't tiles — it's the ground speaking its hero line: **"Last time the moon, the tide, and the cold lined up like this at Sewells Point: October 22, 1961. Seven times in 76 years."** Tap that date and you don't leave — you *fall through five decades* and the same card lands wearing 1961's numbers, with what followed and the odds attached.

Every idea below either builds the camera, builds the sentence, or gives the landing consequence. Nothing that is "another card in the rail" gets built. That's the whole bet: no tool on Earth is a fact-only memory you physically fall into. That's the one-of-a-kind.

## 4. DO NOT BUILD

- **The Water Ledger as pitched.** Its demo lies three times: data is monthly not daily, the current pipe is dead (zero 2026 rows), and VA has 2 gauges. "James River @ Cartersville, 54 July 5ths" does not exist in your archive. Park until a VA backfill top-up; demo-state honesty isn't good enough for the second thing you ship.
- **Flight Report now.** BirdCast is dark until August and the hero moment can't render in July. Build it in-season, in an afternoon, as depth — never as the signature.
- **The Front Ledger's 76-year birds claim.** Radar starts 2021. The reframed census-plus-radar-era version is fine *later*; the pitched version is impossible and shipping a softened ghost of it now dilutes the 1961 moment.
- **Three separate geography canvases.** Who Stood Here, Fly-to, and Dots-below-the-clouds are all good — and all become *layers on the Bloom's canvas* after item 5. One camera, one projection; layers land on it in order. Building any of them first means building the map twice.
- **Any summed score, convergence number, or LLM sentence on the hero path.** The predictor is demolished and stays dead. The eye does the recognizing; the story button stays opt-in.
- **The WebGL globe.** The orbit-to-creek promise is real but the SVG camera delivers the *feel* this quarter. Reach for MapLibre when the county floor is alive and asking for it.
- **One more stat tile, anywhere.** Every new element ships as a dated sentence, a named place, a camera move, or a ledger row with a denominator — or it doesn't ship. That's the line between this and every dashboard template on the internet.

The sequence in one breath: **the sentence (1), the fall (2, 3), the consequence (4), the ground (5), the ritual (6), the gift (7).** Items 1+2 start tonight, in parallel.
